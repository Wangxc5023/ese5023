Keeling Curve

Charles David Keeling directed a program to measure the concentrations of CO2 in the atmosphere that continued without interruption from the late 1950’s through the present. This program, operated out of Scripps Institution of Oceanography, is responsible for the Mauna Loa record, which is almost certainly the best-known icon illustrating the impact of humanity on the planet as a whole. 1

We will use monthly CO2 data measured from Mauna Loa in this section.

To begin with, download the co2_mm_mlo.csv file from here. And save it to your working directory.
